Day 1: Monday 10 November 2025

8:00am - 9.30am Registration, welcome & introduction
Speakers, workshop organisers and participants.
9:30am - 3:00pm Data linkages, standardisation, management & documentation. 3:00pm - 5:00pm Interactive, hands-on group discussion session using HIV/AIDS dataset, followed by Q&A.
Prof. Jim Todd, Ms. Leah Sanga, Mr. Frank Mapendo, & Mr. Tathagata Bhattacharjee
Topics include data standardisation, data linkages using deterministic, probabilistic and machine learning approaches & documentation.

Day 2: Tuesday 11 November 2025

8:25am - 3:00pm Survival analysis techniques 3:00pm - 5:00pm Interactive, hands-on group discussion session using cancer dataset, followed by Q&A.
Dr. Sophia Kagoye, Dr. Wende Safari, Dr. Innocent Mboya, & Dr. Justine Nasejje
Topics include survival analysis basics, non-parametric methods, proportional hazards modelling, & competing risks.

Day 3: Wednesday 12 November 2025

9:00am - 4:00pm Extended network data linkage meeting for the AMS networking grant partners

Day 4: Thursday 13 November 2025

8:25am - 3:00pm Time-to-event and longitudinal data joint modelling approaches. 3:00pm - 5:00pm Interactive, hands-on group discussion session using HIV/AIDS dataset, followed by Q&A.
Dr. Jacqueliene Materu & Dr. Njeru Njagi
Topics include utility of joint modelling, formulating joint models & different parameterisations of the joint models.

Day 5: Friday 14 November 2025

8.25am - 3:00pm Handling missing data using multiple imputation methods in HIV/AIDS and cancer data. 3:00pm - 5:00pm Interactive, hands-on group discussion session using HIV/AIDS dataset, followed by Q&A.
Dr. Neema Mosha, Dr. Phillip Ayeko, & Dr. Njeru Njagi
Topics include missing data mechanisms, multiple imputation theory, fully conditional specification approach & building the imputation model and compatibility problems.
