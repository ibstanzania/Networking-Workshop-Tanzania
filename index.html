About Us

At the International Biometric Society (IBS) Tanzania Region, we empower statisticians, epidemiologists and data professionals in Tanzania and sub-Saharan Africa through a workshop series focused on analysing routinely collected real-world data using open-source software such as R.

Academy of Medical Science partners in Tanzania are currently funded by the the Academy of Medical Sciences (AMS) Networking Grant for a project titled "An international and interdisciplinary network for analysing routine HIV/AIDS and cancer registry data in Tanzania". This grant supports the organisation of the third IBS Tanzania Region networking workshop.

AMS Network Partners & IBS Tanzania Workshop

Our workshops are designed to capacitate individuals working with routine electronic health records data (i.e., HIV/AIDS and cancer data) with hands-on practical skills in data standardisation, linkages, management and analysisas well as creating flourishing opportunities to network with national and international experts.

Join us for a 5-day workshop (10-14 November 2025) featuring exclusive lectures and impactful networking opportunities with Biostatistical methodologists in HIV/AIDS and cancer research from Tanzania, Kenya, the UK, and South Africa.

By the end of the workshop, participants are expected to:

➊ Use statistical and machine learning methods for data linkage, processing & documentation using R and Python.

➋ Apply survival analysis techniques for analysing HIV/AIDS and cancer data.

➌ Joint model longitudinal and time-to-event HIV/AIDS and cancer ouctomes.

➍ Handle missing data using robust multiple imputation framework.

➎ Critically evaluate analytical methods to use in cancer epidemiology, HIV/AIDS reasearch and public health research questions.

➏ Acquire crucial skills for reproducible research using R Markdown and version control (GIT).

➐ Engage in professional networking with peers, national and international experts.

We warmly welcome ethuisiastic researchers (just like us!) with statistics or data science background to advance their skills in using open-source statistical software R and Python for analysing routine HIV/AIDS and cancer data.

To fully benefit from the workshop, participants should bring a personal laptop with the required software pre-installed, as detailed in the Download section.
