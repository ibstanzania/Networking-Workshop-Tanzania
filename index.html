About Us

Since 2022, the International Biometric Society (IBS) Tanzania Region has been empowering statisticians and data professionals in Tanzania and sub-Saharan Africa through workshop series on analysis of routinely collected data using R software.

The call to the third workshop is now open. This workshop is designed to equip individuals working with routine HIV/AIDS and cancer data with hands-on practical skills in data management and analysis, while also creating opportunities to network with national and international experts.

Join us for a 5-day workshop uniting Tanzanian expertise with global leaders in biostatistics — dynamic talks, exclusive lectures, and impactful networking. This year, we are excited to welcome Dr. Justine Nasejje and Dr. Edmund Njeru Njagi, bringing global expertise to our workshop in HIV/AIDS and cancer data analysis.

By the end of the workshop, participants are expected to:

➊ Apply statistical and machine learning methods for data linkage, processing, and documentation techniques using R and Python to improve the quality and usability of routinely collected HIV/AIDS and cancer datasets.

➋ Use survival analysis techniques for analysing HIV/AIDS and cancer data.

➌ Conduct joint modelling approaches in R to analyse longitudinal and time-to-event HIV/AIDS and cancer data.

➍ Handle missing data appropriately using robust multiple imputation framework.

➎ Critically evaluate analytical methods to use in cancer epidemiology, HIV/AIDS reasearch and public health research.

➏ Acquire crucial skills for reproducible research using R Markdown and version control (GIT), with application to cancer and HIV/AIDs data, research projects, and other relevant areas.

➐ Engage in professional networking with peers, national and international experts to stimulate environment for future collaborations in Tanzania and beyond.

We warmly welcome ethuisiastic researchers (just like us!) with diverse academic and professional backgrounds who are eager to advance their skills in using open-source statistical software R and Python for analysing routine HIV/AIDS and cancer data.

To fully benefit from the workshop, participants should bring a personal laptop with the required software pre-installed, as detailed in the Download section.
