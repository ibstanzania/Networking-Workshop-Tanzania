About Us

We are the Academy of Medical Science Network (AMS) Partners and the International Biometric Society (IBS) Tanzania Region. Our vision is to facilitate linkage, management, and analysis of routinely collected HIV/AIDS and cancer registry data in Tanzania. The purpose is to generate high-quality evidence to identify high-risk populations and develop tailored potential interventions, particularly for people living in underserved communities in Tanzania.

We are currently funded by the AMS Networking Grant for a project titled "An international and interdisciplinary network for analysing routine HIV/AIDS and cancer registry data in Tanzania" . Through this project, we aim to organise two IBS Tanzania Region networking workshops.

AMS Network Partners Workshops 1 & 2

We run Workshop 1 successfully focused on building sustainable partnership to strengthen HIV and cancer data linkages by integrating hospital CTC and population-based cancer registry data at Bugando Medical Centre, Tanzania.

Join us for Workshop 2, a sequel (10-14 November 2025) featuring exclusive lectures and impactful networking opportunities with Biostatistical methodologists in HIV/AIDS and cancer research from Tanzania, Kenya, the UK, and South Africa.

By the end of Workshop 2, participants are expected to:

➊ Use statistical and machine learning methods for data linkage, processing & documentation using R and Python.

➋ Apply survival analysis techniques for analysing HIV/AIDS &and cancer data.

➌ Joint model longitudinal and time-to-event HIV/AIDS & cancer ouctomes.

➍ Handle missing data using robust multiple imputation framework.

➎ Critically evaluate analytical methods to use in cancer epidemiology, HIV/AIDS & public health research questions.

➏ Acquire crucial skills for reproducible research using R Markdown & version control (GIT).

➐ Engage in professional networking with peers, national & international experts.

We warmly welcome ethuisiastic researchers (just like us!) with statistics or data science background to advance their skills in using open-source statistical software R and Python for analysing routine HIV/AIDS and cancer data.

To fully benefit from the workshop, participants should bring personal laptops with the required software pre-installed, as detailed in the Download section.
